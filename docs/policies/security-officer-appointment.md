# Security Officer Appointment & Responsibilities

## TLDR

Formally designates the Information Security Officer (ISO) and HIPAA Security Official for Helix Stax. Defines core responsibilities, delegation matrix for scaling (1 to 10 staff), reporting structure, and time commitment. Required by HIPAA, ISO 27001, and SOC 2. Approved by CEO.

---

## Purpose

This document formalizes the appointment and responsibilities of the Information Security Officer (ISO) and HIPAA Privacy/Security Official, ensuring Helix Stax meets regulatory requirements for designated security leadership.

## Scope

- The Information Security Officer role and all associated responsibilities
- All ISMS governance and HIPAA compliance oversight
- Delegation planning as the organization scales

---

## Policy Statements

### 1. Formal Appointment

Effective March 23, 2026, **Wakeem Williams** is formally appointed as the Information Security Officer (ISO) and the HIPAA Security Official for Helix Stax. In this capacity, the appointee is granted authority to implement, manage, and oversee all information security policies, procedures, and technical controls required to maintain compliance with SOC 2, ISO 27001, and HIPAA.

This appointment remains in effect until a formal successor is named or the organizational structure is modified.

**Accepted By:** __________________________
**Title:** CEO & Information Security Officer

### 2. Core Responsibilities

1. **Policy Maintenance:** Annual review and update of the ISMS and HIPAA Security Rule policies
2. **Incident Response Lead:** Primary point of contact for security breaches, coordinating containment and managing notification obligations
3. **Audit Coordination:** Lead liaison for SOC 2 Type I/II examinations and ISO 27001 certification audits
4. **Risk Assessment:** Conducting annual Security Risk Analyses (SRA) as required by HIPAA and ISO 27001 Clause 6.1
5. **Training Oversight:** Ensuring all staff complete security awareness and HIPAA privacy training upon hire and annually
6. **Vendor Management:** Reviewing Business Associate Agreements (BAAs) and assessing the security posture of critical sub-processors (Cloudflare, Backblaze)

### 3. Delegation Matrix for Growth (1 to 10 Staff)

| Function | Current (Solo) | Scaling (5-10 Staff) |
|----------|---------------|---------------------|
| Strategy and Governance | CEO/ISO | CEO/ISO |
| Technical Implementation | CEO/ISO | Lead DevOps Engineer |
| Access Reviews | CEO/ISO | Operations Manager |
| Log Monitoring | CEO/ISO | Managed SOC or Lead Engineer |
| HR Security (Onboarding) | CEO/ISO | Operations/HR Lead |
| Physical Security | CEO/ISO | Facilities/Office Manager |

### 4. Reporting Structure and Time Commitment

- **Reporting:** While CEO and ISO are currently the same individual, "Executive Management Review" meetings must be documented quarterly per ISO 27001 Clause 9.3. Minutes record the ISO's report to the CEO.
- **Time Commitment:**
  - Steady State: 4-6 hours per month for log reviews, access audits, and vendor checks
  - Audit/Assessment Windows: 10-15 hours per week during active audit cycles
  - Scaling Trigger: Once the firm reaches 7 employees, a part-time Compliance Coordinator should be appointed for evidence collection
